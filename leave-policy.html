Holiday and Leave Policy

Purpose: The purpose of Holiday and Leave policy is to lay guidelines regarding various holidays and leaves in a financial year (April to March).
  • Public Holidays
  • Earned Leave
  • Sick Leave
  • Recreational Leave
  • Maternity Leave
  • Leave Without Pay
  • Compensatory Leave
Eligibility: This policy is applicable to all the employees of the company working in India.

Guidelines for Holiday and Leave Policy:

Public Holidays:
  • You are entitled to 10 public holidays in a financial year. If you are required to work on a public holiday as an outcome of business requirements, will be entitled to a compensatory leave in lieu of the same.

Earned Leave:
  • You are entitled to 12 earned leaves in financial year. These leaves will be credited as 1 day at the end of each month (adjusted on pro-rata basis for new joiners).
  • Employees will be eligible for earned leave only after completion of probationary period.
  • Not more than 5 earned leaves can be carried forward at the end of financial year.
  • Additional earned leave will be forfeited.
  • For existing employees carried forward earned leave balance from previous year will be updated in the month of April.
  • If you are on leave for a period exceeding your entitlement, appropriate deductions will be made from your salary.
  • If you terminate your employment and have a negative leave balance, the balance will be adjusted against full and final settlement by company.
  • Earned leave need to be charged under Earned Leave (EL) category in Timesheet.
  • For planned leave(s), two weeks prior notice must be given to the reporting manager (email approval mandatory). Any kind of leave can be granted or refused depending upon the business demands. Leave of absence from work without proper approval will call for disciplinary action.
  • In case of emergency when leave cannot be applied in advance, telephonic intimation to the immediate reporting manager should be done.
Sick Leave:
  • You are entitled to 6 sick leaves in financial year on medical grounds. These leaves will be credited as 0.5 day at the end of the month.
  • Employees will be eligible for sick leaves only after completion of probationary period.
  • Sick leave needs to be charged under Sick Leave (SL) category in Timesheet.
  • If you are availing sick leave, then you must call up and inform your reporting manager minimum six hours before the start of the next working day. However, in case of unforeseen circumstances, you or your immediate family member may inform the reporting manager within 24 hours.
Recreational Leave:
  • You are entitled to 2 recreational leave in a financial year to have quality time with your family.
  • Recreational leave will be credited at once after the completion of probation period.
  • Recreational leave needs to be charged under Recreational Leave (RL) category in Timesheet.
  • Two weeks prior intimation must be given to reporting manager and is subject to be availed on approval only.
Maternity Leave:
  • All confirmed employees shall be entitled for maternity leave as per Maternity Benefit Act 2016, with full pay for a period of continuous 26 weeks for each pregnancy up to a maximum of 2 confinements.
  • Leave taken for prenatal treatment for the first 7 months of pregnancy will be considered as normal leave not maternity leave.
  • You can only avail maternity leave once it is approved by the reporting manager.
  • Employee proceeding on maternity leave must also submit doctor’s certificate to HR.
  • Maternity Leave needs to be charged under Maternity Leave (ML) in Timesheet.
Leave in case of adoption of child or birth through surrogacy
  • In case of adoption of child or child birth through surrogacy, you are entitled to 12 weeks of leave.
  • These Leaves can be availed on when the child has actually started living with the parents.
  • In the above-mentioned case leave must be applied at least 6 weeks before the date of adoption.
  • All legal certificate and required documents must be submitted to HR.
  • Surrogacy Leave needs to be charged under Surrogacy Leave (SURL) in Timesheet.
Leave without pay
  • You can avail leave without pay in case existing leave balance is exhausted and you are in need of leave due to unforeseen circumstances.
  • In case no approval taken for leave without pay, such absence of employee will be considered as Leave of Absence from work.
  • Disciplinary action will be taken in case of absence without approval.
  • No salary would be given to employee for the days leave without pay is availed.
  • A maximum of 30 days leave without pay can be availed by an employee.
  • You can only avail this leave after the approval from reporting manager.
  • Leave Without Pay needs to be charged under Unpaid Leave (UL) in Timesheet.
Compensatory Leave
  • You are eligible for compensatory off when you have worked on an important assignment on any of the national/declared off day.
  • Approval to work on national/declared holiday must be taken from immediate reporting manager.
  • Compensatory leave balance will be visible in Leave Management Tool.
  • Compensatory leave needs to be charged under Compensatory Leave (CL) in Timesheet.

Cancellation of leave

  • Approved leave can be cancelled depending upon high priority business demand or employee can also cancel the leave in case of cancellation of planned leave.
  • Leave balance will be updated accordingly by HR

Extension of leave

  • In case of extension of leave due to any unforeseen circumstances, you must have to inform reporting manager in advance; once extension of leave is approved by reporting manager than only it will be considered as earned leave else will be consider as absence from work and disciplinary action will be taken against the employee.
  • Leave extended without permission will be treated as loss of pay.

Leave calculation on resignation/termination

  • In case you have resigned from the services, earned leave will be calculated till the last working day of the employee and will be paid in full and final settlement to the employee.
  • In case employment has been terminated by the company on the basis of breach of company’s Code of Conduct or for any unbearable reason, leave encashment won’t be considered.

Few points to remember while availing leave

  • An employee shall not proceed on leave until unless leave has been approved by reporting manager.
  • If an employee is absent continuously for 7 days beyond sanctioned leave with no information, in this case employee shall be considered to have left her employment on one’s free will. HR will take action in this case. Warning Letter will be issued to the employee if she does not return within 7 days of expiry of sanctioned leave. If there is no response from employee within 3 days of issuing warning letter, final termination letter will be issued.
  • In case of prolonged illness or leave of absence from work an employee is supposed to inform the immediate reporting manager at regular interval about their condition and most probable date of return. In absence of any communication from employee serious action can be taken by the company.
  • Leave without approval will be considered as leave without pay.

Revision of the policy

  • The company reserves the right to revise, modify any or all clauses of this policy depending upon demand of business.
